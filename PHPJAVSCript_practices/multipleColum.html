Now is the winter of our discontent made glorious by this sun of York; And all the clouds that lour'd upon our house in the deep bosom of the ocean buried. Now are out brows bound with victorious wreaths; Our Brusied arms hung up for monuments; Our stern alarms changed to merry meeting, our dreadful marches tp delightful measures. Grim-visaged war hath smooth'd his wrinkled front; and now, instead of mounting barded steeds. to fright the souls of fearful adversaries, He capers nimbly in a lady's chamber To the lascivuos pleasing of a lute.
